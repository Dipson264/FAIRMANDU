Visit the best place in Kathmandu

Discover:

Kathmandu, the vibrant capital of Nepal, is a city rich in history, culture, and spirituality. Nestled in a valley surrounded by the Himalayas, it is home to ancient temples, stunning stupas, and lively marketplaces that offer a glimpse into Nepal’s rich traditions. From the sacred Hindu temple of Pashupatinath to the serene Buddhist stupas of Swayambhunath and Boudhanath, the city is a blend of diverse religious influences. The bustling streets of Thamel, the historic palaces of Durbar Squares, and the breathtaking views from Chandragiri Hills make Kathmandu an unforgettable destination for travelers. Whether you are seeking spiritual enlightenment, historical exploration, or adventure, Kathmandu has something for everyone.

Hidden treasures of Kathmandu

Pullahari Monastery

Pullahari Monastery sits atop a ridge in Kathmandu's northern hills, the highest of three monasteries in the area.

Jamchen Vijaya Stupa

Jamchen Vijaya Stupa is a newly built beautiful monument on the top of Budhanilkantha Hill. From the top, you can see the panoramic view of Kathmandu Valley.

White Gumba (Kopan Monastery)

Seto Gumba is a cultural site full of natural beauty, Tibetean architecture & culture.It is surrounded by lush green valley.

Shivapuri National Park (Dhap Dam)

Dhap Dam is a stunning man-made lake on the edge of Shivapuri National Park.

Major tourist attractions

Swayambhunath

Located on a hill in the western part of Kathmandu, Swayambhunath is one of the oldest and most significant Buddhist stupas in Nepal. The site is famous for its white dome, golden spire, and watchful Buddha eyes overlooking the city. Monkeys roam freely around the temple, adding to its charm. The hilltop location offers breathtaking views of the Kathmandu Valley, making it a great spot for photography and meditation.

Scam Risk Rating: 3/10

Pashupatinath

Situated on the banks of the Bagmati River in eastern Kathmandu, Pashupatinath is one of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple complex features intricately carved wooden pagodas and is famous for the open-air cremation ghats. The evening aarti (ritual) is a mesmerizing experience with chanting and fire rituals. Foreigners are not allowed inside the main temple but can explore the surroundings.

Scam Risk Rating: 5/10

Boudhanath Stupa

Located in northeastern Kathmandu, Boudhanath is one of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world and a major pilgrimage site. Surrounded by monasteries and Tibetan shops, the area has a peaceful spiritual ambiance, with monks and devotees circumambulating the stupa while spinning prayer wheels. It is an excellent place to experience Tibetan Buddhist culture.

Scam Risk Rating: 2/10

Kathmandu Durbar Square

Situated in the center of Kathmandu near Basantapur, this UNESCO-listed site was once the royal palace of the Malla and Shah kings. The square is home to numerous temples, courtyards, and the Kumari Ghar, where the living goddess Kumari resides. While some buildings were damaged in the 2015 earthquake, restoration is ongoing, and the area remains a cultural hub.

Scam Risk Rating: 6/10

Patan Durbar Square

Located in Lalitpur, about 5 km south of Kathmandu, Patan Durbar Square is known for its exquisite Newari architecture and artistic heritage. The square features ancient palaces, temples, and the Patan Museum, which showcases Nepal’s rich cultural history. It is a quieter alternative to Kathmandu Durbar Square, with fewer crowds and a more relaxed atmosphere.

Scam Risk Rating: 4/10

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Situated about 13 km east of Kathmandu, Bhaktapur Durbar Square is one of the best-preserved historical sites in Nepal. It is known for its traditional Newari culture, pottery squares, and stunning temples like the Nyatapola Temple. The area has a medieval charm, with narrow alleyways, wooden carvings, and bustling marketplaces.

Scam Risk Rating: 5/10

Thamel

Located in central Kathmandu, Thamel is the city’s main tourist hub, packed with souvenir shops, restaurants, bars, and trekking gear stores. It is the best place to find travel agencies, book tours, and experience Kathmandu’s nightlife. The streets are lively and colorful, making it a fun place to explore, but it is also the most touristy area.

Scam Risk Rating: 7/10

Garden of Dreams

Situated near Thamel, the Garden of Dreams is a beautifully designed European-style garden built during the Rana era. It offers a peaceful retreat from the chaos of Kathmandu, with well-maintained lawns, fountains, and pavilions. It’s a great place to relax with a book or enjoy a quiet afternoon.

Scam Risk Rating: 2/10

Chandragiri Hills

Located about 16 km southwest of Kathmandu, Chandragiri Hills offer stunning panoramic views of the Himalayas and the city below. Visitors can take a cable car ride to the top, where there is a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and plenty of space for sightseeing and photography. The clean air and scenic landscapes make it a refreshing escape from the busy city.

Scam Risk Rating: 3/10
